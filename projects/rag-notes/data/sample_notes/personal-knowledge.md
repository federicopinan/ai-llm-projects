# Personal Knowledge Management

Personal Knowledge Management, or PKM, is the practice of capturing what you
learn, organizing it so it can be found again, and turning it into something
useful. Good PKM compounds: every note makes the next note easier to write.

## The capture habit

The hardest part of PKM is not the tooling — it is the habit of writing
things down. A practical rule: if a thought will matter in a week, capture
it now. Captured ideas can be refactored later; forgotten ones are gone.

## Linking over folders

Folders are a brittle way to organize knowledge. A note about caching
strategies might belong under "backend", "performance", or "databases", and
the right answer changes as the project evolves.

Linking notes to other notes — the approach popularized by tools like
Obsidian and Roam — keeps the structure flexible. A note finds its home by
what it points to, not by where it sits.

## Progressive summarization

Notes should be rewritten over time. A first capture is raw: a quote, a
screenshot caption, a half-formed idea. Later passes promote the most
useful parts to bold, then to a one-line summary at the top. The most
durable knowledge ends up near the title, easy to scan months later.

## Retrieval matters more than capture

A notes system you cannot search is a journal. The job of PKM is to bring
the right note back at the right time. Search covers keyword recall. For
the questions you cannot phrase — "what did I read about caching last
month?" — semantic search and retrieval-augmented generation extend the
reach of plain search.

## Markdown as the lingua franca

Plain markdown files survive every tool change. They diff cleanly, version
control cleanly, and render in any editor. Keeping notes as markdown means
the knowledge outlives the application that wrote it.

## Review as a system

PKM is not just writing. A weekly review — looking at recent notes,
re-linking, archiving dead ends — is what turns a stream of captures into a
working second brain.

## A working definition

A personal knowledge base is durable when its owner can answer questions
about their past thinking without remembering every detail.
